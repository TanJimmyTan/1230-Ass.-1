Sydney - Barangaroo Reimagination

Barangaroo is an urban tramsformation that attempts to create space from old docking wharves to accomodate the rapid expansion and growth of Sydney. Barangaroo attempts to provide commercial, retail, and living spaces while also creating an icon to attract tourists and further the tourism industry in Australia.



There are 4 main parts to this project. The International Towers, which provides more commercial space than the Sydney CBD, Barangaroo Wharf, which creates more points of entry and commute options into the city, the Crown Casino, a new 6 star hotel casino, and Barangaroo Reserve, an artificial open headlands area.



International Towers

The international Towers represent the commercial aspect of the Barangaroo transformation. The international Towers provide a huge amount commercial space that aimed at being used as a global business site, rather than a local commercial space likee the Sydney CBD. Not only is it a business space, these towers act as an icon of Barangaroo, 3 imposing towers set as the backdrop of Sydney.



Barangaroo Wharf

Barangaroo Wharf is a new wharf that aims to keep the sleek aesthetic of the towers, and provide a secondary commute option for people traveling around the city. Not only does it increase the flow of people into Barangaroo, it also means that the spread of people within the city is decreased, reducing traffic within Sydney.



Crown Casino

The Crown Casino is the 2nd legal gambling institution wihtin Sydney, the first being the Star Hotel. The Crown Casino however, is intended to be the first 6 star hotel in Sydney, and aims to become an icon to attract interest in travelling to Australia.



Barangaroo Reserve

Barangaroo Reserve is an aritificial headlands incuded within the plans of the Barangaroo Project to improve the environment of Sydney. Having completed construction in 2015, this reserve is a foreshore park with intense views of Sydney's iconic harbour, extensive trails for both walking and cycling, picnic spots, and many more relaxing outdoors recreational activities.



Project Evaluation

The project itself can be evaluated based on whether or not each aspect was successful in its endeavor. To begin, the aim of the International Towers was to accomodate for the growing commercial state within Sydney, and reduce traffic flow within the city. By moving a large amount of comemrcial traffic into Barangaroo, traffic congestion within Sydney has decreased, and with a commercial space larger than the Sydney CBD, the International Towers have achieved their goal of accomodating for the growwing commerce within Sydney. Barangaroo Wharf provides new entry points into the city, and also reduces traffic flow around the city, adding to the goal of reducing traffic in Sydney.
